How to get better in Communicating

Communication is one of the most powerful skills you can develop—it shapes relationships, builds opportunities, and even defines leadership. Whether you're speaking in public, negotiating a deal, or simply having a meaningful conversation, mastering communication can set you apart.

Through my own journey of learning from my mistakes, I have discovered some effective ways to improve my communication skills. Here’s how you can stay prepared and avoid making the same errors:

Talk in Your Mind Before It Comes Out of Your Mouth

I've been practicing this for a while now, and it has significantly improved the quality of my conversations. Before I say something out loud, I first speak it in my mind. It wasn’t easy at first—it took time to develop this habit—but now, I can see how much it has helped me communicate more effectively.

Thinking before speaking allows you to refine your words, ensuring they are impactful, clear, and purposeful. It prevents unnecessary rambling, miscommunication, and those awkward moments when you wish you had phrased something better. But won’t this make me sound slow or boring? Absolutely not. Take a look at great influencers, CEOs, and leaders—they all pause before responding. They carefully process their thoughts and deliver responses with precision. They don’t rush; they value clarity over speed. And that leads me to my next point.

---

Slow Down, Stay Calm, and Then Speak

Have you ever blurted out an answer too quickly, only to realize you made a mistake in grammar or choice of words? I have—many times. And I can tell you from experience that a fast response doesn’t always mean a good response.

I remember a speech competition where I rushed through my words, thinking speed would make me sound confident. But in reality, it led to fumbled sentences and lost impact. Had I paused for just a second, I could have crafted a stronger message. Taking a moment to process your thoughts shows confidence and depth. It signals to your audience that you are thoughtful and deliberate. A simple phrase like, "That’s a great question—let me take a moment to think" can work wonders. It not only buys you time but also increases the listener’s anticipation of your response. So, don’t rush. Speak with intention, and your words will carry more weight.

---

Listen Actively and Silently

Being a good communicator isn’t just about speaking well—it’s also about listening effectively. When someone else is talking, be fully present. Listen not just to respond, but to understand.

When you truly engage in a conversation and show genuine interest in what the other person is saying, your responses become more insightful and relevant. This leaves a lasting impression and makes people appreciate talking to you. So, listen more than you speak. Silence can be powerful.

---

Keep Your Tone Moderate and Your Words Simple

Your tone matters as much as your words. Ever had someone tell you to "lower your voice" or "speak up"? It’s a sign that your tone isn’t aligned with the situation.

Before speaking, observe your environment and the people around you. Adjust your voice accordingly, but always keep it at a moderate, steady level. A calm and composed tone instantly makes you sound more confident and credible. And when it comes to words—simplicity wins. Some people think using complex vocabulary makes them sound intelligent, but in reality, it often creates confusion. The best communicators use clear, straightforward language that everyone can understand.

---

Speaking well is an art—one that requires thoughtfulness, patience, and practice. When you think before speaking, slow down, listen actively, and keep things simple, your words will naturally carry more power. Try it. You’ll be amazed at how much more impactful your conversations become.
